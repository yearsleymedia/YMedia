About You

Don't just say your job title — describe who you help, how you help them, and what makes you different. The more specific you are, the more your content will feel personal and targeted rather than generic.

2
Your Audience
Who are you talking to?

Think about who you most enjoy working with and who gets the best results from what you do. Consider their age, situation, lifestyle, and what they're trying to achieve. The more clearly you can picture this person, the better your content will speak directly to them.

What keeps your ideal client stuck? What have they tried that hasn't worked? What do they say to themselves at the end of a hard day? Content that speaks directly to someone's real frustration is the content that stops the scroll.

What does life look like for your client after working with you? Think beyond the practical outcome — how do they feel? What can they do that they couldn't before? This is what makes people want to buy, and it's what makes your content compelling.
